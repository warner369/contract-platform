# Test Contract: Software Development Services Agreement

This contract is between TechVenture Ltd ("Client") and CodeCraft Solutions Pty Ltd ("Provider"), collectively referred to as the "Parties", for the development of a cloud-based platform known as the "Platform".

## 1. Definitions

"Confidential Information" means any information disclosed by either Party that is marked as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure. "Intellectual Property" means all patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets, and other intellectual property rights in the Platform and Deliverables. "Deliverables" means the software, documentation, and materials developed by the Provider under this Agreement. "Services" means the software development, testing, deployment, and support services described in Schedule A. "Term" means the period from the Effective Date until termination in accordance with Clause 9.

## 2. Scope of Services

The Provider shall develop the Platform in accordance with the specification set out in Schedule A. The Provider shall assign a dedicated project manager who shall be the primary point of contact for the Client. The Provider may subcontract portions of the Services to third parties provided that the Provider remains responsible for the quality and timely delivery of all subcontracted work. The Client shall provide timely access to necessary systems, data, and personnel as reasonably required for the Provider to perform the Services.

## 3. Payment Terms

The Client shall pay the Provider a fixed fee of one hundred and fifty thousand dollars ($150,000) for the Services, payable in three equal instalments upon execution of this Agreement, delivery of the Beta Version, and delivery of the Final Version respectively. All invoices are payable within thirty (30) days of receipt. Late payments shall accrue interest at the rate of two percent (2%) per month. The Provider may suspend Services if any invoice remains unpaid for more than sixty (60) days.

## 4. Intellectual Property

All Intellectual Property developed during the Term shall be owned by the Client upon payment in full of all fees due under this Agreement. Until full payment, the Provider retains ownership of all Intellectual Property. The Provider grants the Client a non-exclusive, non-transferable licence to use any pre-existing Provider tools or frameworks incorporated into the Deliverables. The Client may not modify, reverse engineer, or distribute the Provider's pre-existing tools or frameworks without prior written consent.

## 5. Confidentiality

Each Party shall maintain the confidentiality of the other's Confidential Information and shall not disclose it to any third party without prior written consent. The receiving Party shall use the same degree of care to protect Confidential Information as it uses to protect its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care. Confidential Information shall not include information that: becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving Party; was already known to the receiving Party at the time of disclosure; is independently developed by the receiving Party without reference to the Confidential Information; or is rightfully obtained from a third party without restriction on disclosure.

## 6. Warranties

The Provider warrants that the Services shall be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards. The Provider warrants that the Deliverables will not infringe any third-party intellectual property rights. The Provider does not warrant that the Platform will be error-free or operate without interruption. The Client warrants that it has the right to provide any materials, data, or content to the Provider for incorporation into the Platform. The Provider's total liability for breach of warranty shall not exceed the fees paid by the Client in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim.

## 7. Limitation of Liability

Neither Party shall be liable for any indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages, including loss of profits, data, business opportunities, or goodwill, regardless of whether such damages were foreseeable. The Provider's total aggregate liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid by the Client during the Term. The limitations in this Clause shall not apply to breaches of Clause 5 (Confidentiality), Clause 4 (Intellectual Property), or liability for death or personal injury caused by negligence.

## 8. Indemnification

The Provider shall indemnify the Client against any third-party claims that the Deliverables infringe intellectual property rights, provided the Client promptly notifies the Provider in writing and cooperates fully in the defence. The Client shall indemnify the Provider against claims arising from the Client's use of the Deliverables in a manner not authorised by this Agreement or in violation of law. The indemnifying Party shall have sole control over the defence and settlement of any indemnified claim.

## 9. Termination

Either Party may terminate this Agreement on thirty (30) days' written notice if the other Party commits a material breach that remains uncured for thirty (30) days after written notice. The Client may terminate for convenience on fifteen (15) days' written notice, provided the Client pays the Provider for all Services performed and expenses incurred through the termination date. Upon termination, the Provider shall return all Client materials and Confidential Information within ten (10) business days. Either Party may terminate immediately if the other Party becomes insolvent, enters bankruptcy proceedings, or ceases to carry on business.

## 10. Force Majeure

Neither Party shall be liable for any delay or failure to perform obligations due to circumstances beyond its reasonable control, including but not limited to natural disasters, war, terrorism, strikes, government actions, pandemics, or failure of internet or telecommunications infrastructure. The affected Party shall notify the other Party within five (5) business days and shall use reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact. If a force majeure event continues for more than ninety (90) days, either Party may terminate this Agreement without liability.

## 11. Dispute Resolution

The Parties shall attempt to resolve any dispute arising under this Agreement through good-faith negotiation within thirty (30) days of written notice of the dispute. If negotiation fails, the dispute shall be submitted to mediation administered by the Australian Dispute Resolution Centre in Sydney. If mediation fails, the dispute shall be resolved by binding arbitration in accordance with the Commercial Arbitration Act 2010 (NSW). The seat of arbitration shall be Sydney, Australia. The language of arbitration shall be English.

## 12. General Provisions

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes all prior discussions, negotiations, and agreements. No amendment to this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by both Parties. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of New South Wales, Australia. Any notice required under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by email to the addresses specified in Schedule B. The Parties confirm that they have read and understood this Agreement and execute it voluntarily. If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

## Schedule A: Platform Specification

The Platform shall be a web-based application enabling contract review and collaboration for small and medium businesses. Key features include AI-powered clause analysis, plain-language explanations, risk assessment, intent-based clause editing, comparison views, and configurable variables. The Platform shall be built using modern web technologies and hosted on cloud infrastructure with automatic scaling. The Provider shall deliver a Beta Version within ninety (90) days and a Final Version within one hundred and fifty (150) days of the Effective Date.
